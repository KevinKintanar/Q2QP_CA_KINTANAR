Mental Health Awareness Week (MHAW)
While MHAW is an annual campaign, it is also a gentle reminder to actively prioritise and nurture our mental health beyond the week itself. We believe that people should connect with and tautoko/support each other, while continuing the kōrero about mental health and wellbeing year-round.
MHAW is our annual campaign that works to help Kiwis understand what boosts their wellbeing and improves mental health. We’ve been running it since 1993 (when not many people wanted to talk publicly about mental health) and we’re so proud of how much its grown.
We pick a new theme every year because there isn’t just one way to wellbeing and there’s no one-size-fits-all approach that will work for everyone in Aotearoa. Hopefully, every new MHAW adds a new skill, tool or strategy to your wellbeing kete to help you every day and to draw on when times are tough.
Pink Shirt Day
Pink Shirt Day is an antibullying campaign that celebrates diversity and creates environments where all people can feel safe, valued and respected. Each year, workplaces, schools, organisations and individuals join the movement to make a stand against bullying.
Bullying is a serious issue in New Zealand. Every year, one in five workers report they have been bullied at work, and Aotearoa has the third-highest rate of school bullying out of 36 OECD countries. People who identify as part of the rainbow community experience higher rates of bullying, and studies show people who are bullied are more likely to experience mental health issues, such as depression, anxiety and even suicidal thoughts.
On Pink Shirt Day we ask Aotearoa to Kōrero Mai, Kōrero Atu, Mauri Tū, Mauri Ora – Speak Up, Stand Together, Stop Bullying!
Pink Shirt Day began in Canada in 2007 when two students took a stand against homophobic bullying after a new year 10 student was harassed and threatened for wearing pink. We’ve been celebrating Pink Shirt Day in Aotearoa since 2009 and the campaign grows stronger and larger every year.
INVOLVEMENTS
    • Cotton On has been the official retail partner for Pink Shirt Day since 2019. In that time they have raised over $2.3 million to support the Mental Health Foundation's bullying elimination efforts.
    • In 2023 the Cotton On Foundation ensured 100% of proceeds from the sale of the Pink Shirt Day tees and tote bags, as well as the sale of all Cotton On Foundation products for the month of May 2023 supported Pink Shirt Day.
    • The Cotton On Foundation has three key pillars - holistic education, mental health and the environment and uses its influence to have positive impact around the world.
    • One of the ways Downer NZ has supported the Mental Health Foundation is by providing an extra boost to our Auckland Marathon runners by matching donations and joining in on race day.
    • The Downer team are also big supporters of our annual campaigns, Pink Shirt Day and Mental Health Awareness Week with activations for their staff that promote positive wellbeing, ensuring every Downer team member has the tools they need to look after their mental heatlh.
